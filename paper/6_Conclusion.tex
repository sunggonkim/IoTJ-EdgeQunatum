\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

This paper presented \EdgeQuantum, a framework enabling variational quantum algorithms on resource-constrained IoT edge devices. Through systematic evaluation on NVIDIA Jetson Orin Nano, we established key findings:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Scalability Limit:} Edge devices with 8GB unified memory achieve a practical maximum of \textbf{26 qubits} (67 million Hilbert space dimensions), with 27 qubits exceeding memory capacity due to intermediate tensor allocations.
    
    \item \textbf{VQE Feasibility:} The transverse-field Ising model is solvable up to 20 qubits with 9.15 seconds per optimization iteration, demonstrating practical ground state estimation on edge hardware.
    
    \item \textbf{QAOA Accuracy:} MaxCut QAOA achieves 100\% approximation ratio for graphs up to 10 nodes with a single variational layer, proving optimal solution discovery is possible on edge devices.
    
    \item \textbf{Energy Efficiency:} Operating at 15W, edge quantum simulation offers 26$\times$ better energy efficiency compared to datacenter GPU alternatives.
\end{enumerate}

These results establish the viability of quantum-inspired edge computing for distributed IoT applications. Future work will explore tensor network methods for scaling beyond 26 qubits and federated quantum learning across edge device clusters.
