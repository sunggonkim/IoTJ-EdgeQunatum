\section{Evaluation}
\label{sec:eval}

We evaluate \EdgeQuantum on real edge hardware to answer three research questions:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{RQ1:} What is the maximum qubit count achievable on edge devices?
    \item \textbf{RQ2:} How does VQE performance scale with system size?
    \item \textbf{RQ3:} Can QAOA find optimal solutions on edge hardware?
\end{itemize}

\subsection{RQ2: VQE Performance}

\begin{equation}
H = -J \sum_{i} Z_i Z_{i+1} - h \sum_i X_i
\end{equation}
with $J=1.0$, $h=0.5$, using a 2-layer hardware-efficient ansatz.

\textbf{Finding 2:} VQE achieves sub-second iteration latency up to 16 qubits, enabling real-time optimization loops. At 20 qubits, each iteration requires 9.15 seconds, limiting practical optimization to low-iteration budgets.

\textbf{Finding 3:} QAOA with a single variational layer (p=1) achieves \textbf{100\% approximation ratio} for graphs up to 10 nodes. At 12 nodes, the ratio decreases to 91.3\%, suggesting that deeper circuits (p$>$1) may be necessary for larger instances.

\subsection{Energy Efficiency}

Operating at 15W, the Jetson Orin Nano achieves:
\begin{itemize}
    \item VQE (16 qubits): 67 gates/sec at 0.22 J/gate
    \item QAOA (10 qubits): Optimal solution in 81.9s = 1.23 kJ total
\end{itemize}

Compared to cloud GPU instances (e.g., A100 at 400W), edge execution offers 26$\times$ better energy efficiency for equivalent qubit counts.
