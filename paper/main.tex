\begin{abstract}
Quantum computing promises exponential speedups for optimization and simulation tasks, yet current quantum hardware remains expensive and centralized in cloud data centers. This paper presents \EdgeQuantum, a framework enabling variational quantum algorithms (VQE, QAOA) on resource-constrained IoT edge devices. Using NVIDIA Jetson Orin Nano with cuQuantum SDK, we demonstrate that edge devices can efficiently simulate quantum circuits up to \textbf{26 qubits} (67 million Hilbert space dimensions) using only 0.5GB GPU memory. Our VQE implementation achieves 1,000+ gate operations per second for Ising model Hamiltonians, while QAOA obtains \textbf{100\% approximation ratio} on MaxCut problems up to 10 qubits. These results establish the viability of quantum-inspired edge computing for distributed IoT applications in smart grids, logistics optimization, and federated quantum learning.
\end{abstract}
